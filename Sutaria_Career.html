During my time in the engineering academy, I have met a couple of industry professionals through workplace field trips and guest speakers on zoom. Here are a few that have inspired me.


Field Trips
Field Trip - LLNL Job Shadow
March 2022

During the Livermore Lawrence National Lab Job Shadow on zoom, I was able to learn more about the lab itself and the types of jobs it offers. Livermore Lawrence National Lab was created in 1952. It currently has 521 facilities with a sister lab in Berkeley. One person that we talked to was Karly Knox. Karly Know started off as an intern with a degree in chemistry. Her current achievements at the laboratory is creating a molecule that turns into safe plastic. We additionally talked with Marc Stearman, who has a bachelors in Computer Science. His current job requires him to work with a lot of chemists, UNIX servers, nuclear weapon stimulations, and the lab’s storage and network evolution. One important advice he gave was how computer science could be applied into many other fields. We also got to take a look at the National Ignition Facility Laser, where the largest laser in the world resides. The light that comes out of this laser is 20 feet long and travels at a 100 trillion of a second. It uses a thermonuclear process, similar to a microwave, except with the use of x-rays. The laser needs a lot of space to amplify the laser and protect the optic. I really appreciated this field trip over zoom, because it opened my eyes to working at the lab as one of my options after college. Previously, I was unaware that all different types of majors can work in the laboratory.

National Ignition Facility Laser

Field Trip - CTE Careers Webinar Series
February 2022

In the biotechnology career presentation over zoom I was able to interact with several individuals about this thriving industry. The presenter, Josie Sette is a director of biotechnology and she talked about how a biotech certificate can get you into a variety of careers such as biomedical technician, manufacturing technician, quality control technician, and animal technician. The definition of biotechnology is “the use of biological knowledge and modern technologies to make useful products from living organisms and biological processes”. Bioengineering, biochemistry, and chemical engineering combine to become biotechnology. This applies to biologics that fight cancer, drug and gene therapies(CRISPR), mRNA vaccines to combat COVID - 19, stem cell research, creating sustainable clothing and leather made from plants and mushrooms and meat substitutes. This presentation opened my eyes to pursuing biotechnology because it can be applied to any field. I additionally went to the computer science and digital media presentation on zoom. The presentor, Ray Kaupp is the regional director of ICT/DM. From this presentation, I learned that technology is required in any company, therefore, there will always be job openings. The bay area is the perfect place to have a job because there are an abundance of tech companies. As someone interested in the community college route, I appreciated learning more about getting an associates in computer science, and then transferring to a UC or CSU.

Josie Sette

Field Trip - JA Entrepreneurship Summit
November 2021

On the Entrepreneurship Summit over zoom, I was able to learn about young individuals who were able to start their own companies. One entrepreneur I found inspiring was Amrit Robins. Mr. Robins is the CEO and founder of Axiom Cloud. His journey as an entrepreneur started when he went to Stanford for political science to solve climate change. The method he took was similar to the scientific method. He started with testing assumptions by interviewing to get more data, created a fundraising plan to create the company, received answers to the assumptions, built prototypes, and lastly got the first employee. With this method, Mr. Robins was able to earn money which attracted customers and more employees. One key point he emphasized was that it isn't hard to start your own business because other companies have already done those things. He additionally said that the hard part of starting your own business is finding a problem and solving it, as well as having dedicated people on the team. Mr. Robins talked about how crucial it is to work hard, to find responsible employees, and have a committed relationship with them so that they stay with the company.

Amrit Robins

Field Trip - Autodesk University
October 2021

This Autodesk University field trip spanned 3 days with several different workshops and keynote speakers. I attended one keynote and two workshops. In the Architecture, Engineering and Construction Design Keynote, a video was played. The ideas presented were based on challenges engineers and architectures faced due to COVID-19. Nicholas Mangon talked about what adjustments were made, such as moving projects to the cloud and using Forged. Forged is a platform that provides a secure foundation for data sharing by connecting designers. Through this keynote, I learned useful information on how people have to adapt and alter their jobs as different hurdles come in the way. One of the two workshops I attended was Dynamo for Civil 3D: Automated Checks of Openformat Road Design Made Easy. In this workshop, I learned more about Autodesk Civil 3D tools. One of the many speakers, Ellisa Ambrassa, is a structural engineer and she discussed the typical process of using Civil 3D. The steps are to first import landXML, prepare the imported surface in the Civil 3D environment, set the design criteria, create horizontal alignments, create vertical profiles, check any design violations, and lastly fix errors and modify according to the design requirements. The last workshop I attended was the Crossroads of Civil 3D and Revit, where I learned more about how Civil 3D and Revit can work together because they both specialize in different things. Civil 3D is mostly good for all types of modeling, except Revit is better when it comes to corridors, walls, and wings on a cross-section. Sometimes both are used to create better designs, like bridges. The abutments and roads are made using Civil 3D and axis alignment parameters are made using Revit.

Nicolas Mangon

Guest Speaker - Gracie Ermi
April 2021

Gracie Ermi is a research software engineer at Vulcan inc in Seattle. Her job requires her to build artificial intelligence to support wildlife research. One of the things she has worked on is automating the process of looking at multiple images and videos for wildlife surveys. These wildlife surveys take an extremely long time as an individual has to look through each media and count the population. So her team worked on elephants specifically and trained a machine learning module to identify the elephants in the picture, which made the lives of scientists easier. She also went into what exactly machine learning is. Machine learning is a technique in artificial intelligence and the goal is to get a computer to do a task by itself without a human. In order to do this, multiple pictures are labeled and a machine learning robot uses those images to identify the difference using all the angles. After looking at the images and their pixels, it could accurately identify in the future what that image is. Ms. Ermi did this specifically with elephants which counted all the elephants in the media by recognizing similarities that it has learned by itself. After doing this, she and her team started doing the same with whales. They were able to track the health of each whale by looking at the pattern on their back which makes them unique.

Gracie Ermi

He discussed several natural language generation machines in a timeline starting from GPT-2 or Generative Pre-trained Transformer, all the way to GPT-3. This talk was highly informational. I got a chance to learn how chat boxes use artificial intelligence to give an output that sounds like a human, rather than a machine. This talk was highly inspirational as well, partly because I too am interested in artificial intelligence and the way a machine can learn from itself. This was definitely an interesting and engaging guest speaker.

Andrea Madotto
