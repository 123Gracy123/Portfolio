Guest Speaker - Andrea Madotto
March 2021

Andrea Madotto is a Ph.D. student in Electronic & Computer Engineering at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology. During the zoom session, he spoke about conversational modeling and language generation. He discussed several natural language generation machines in a timeline starting from GPT-2 or Generative Pre-trained Transformer, all the way to GPT-3. He went into more extensive details about how the GPT-3 works and how it takes in information using a statistical code and gives a certain output depending on what was entered. He additionally addressed how the GPT-3 picks its certain output by going through its database and picking a word that is rated with the highest numerical value. This I found quite fascinating. This talk was highly informational. I got a chance to learn how chat boxes use artificial intelligence to give an output that sounds like a human, rather than a machine. This talk was highly inspirational as well, partly because I too am interested in artificial intelligence and the way a machine can learn from itself. This was definitely an interesting and engaging guest speaker.
